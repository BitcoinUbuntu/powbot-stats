PoW Video Guidelines

How to create qualifying Proof of Work video posts for PoWBoT

What Qualifies

Your video post must meet ALL of these requirements:

  • Show the transaction: Video must capture an actual Bitcoin payment happening
  • Merchant receives sats directly: No fiat settlement services – merchant must receive Bitcoin
  • Merchant paycode/LNURL in post: Must be clearly typed in the written post text (not just shown in video). If multiple paycodes are included, the merchant's must appear first
  • BTCMap.org link required: Must include a link to the merchant's BTCMap.org profile in your post
  • Permanent merchant location: Must be an established business with a fixed physical location (no mobile vendors, delivery services, or temporary stands)
  • Include #spedn hashtag: Your post must include the hashtag #spedn
  • Valid on X & Nostr: Posts on either platform qualify
Submission Limit: Only ONE submission per day per merchant-platform combination.

This means:
✅ Submit as many different merchants as you want each day
✅ Post the same merchant video on BOTH X and Nostr (both submissions qualify)
❌ Post the same merchant twice on the same platform in one day

Example: Post about Merchant A on X and Nostr today? Yes! Post about Merchants A, B, and C on X today? Yes! Post about Merchant A twice on X today? No.

Keep it short: Videos should be 45-60 seconds maximum. Show only what's necessary – we don't need to see you eat the entire meal. Edit your clips to the essential moments.
Proper formatting: Crop your videos correctly. Do not edit portrait videos onto a horizontal canvas – keep videos in their native orientation.

What Doesn't Qualify

  • Fiat settlement: Merchant receiving fiat through off-ramps
  • No verifiable location: Random sellers, unidentifiable locations, temporary stands, street vendors
  • Mobile services: Taxis, delivery services, or any mobile merchants (too difficult to verify location)
  • Random people at meetups: Sending sats to individuals who aren't merchants
  • Salary payments: Paying employees is not a PoW video. Employees should spend their salaries at merchants instead
  • Talking without showing: Describing what you did without on-camera evidence of the transaction
  • No context videos: Videos filmed outside your circular economy project or without identifying location
  • Project-owned shops: Spending at a demo shop your project set up, or at stores your project owns/operates
  • Unclear transactions: If the video doesn't clearly show sats being spent, it won't count
